Helping a Victim-Survivor

When someone tells you they are experiencing violence, your main job is to listen and believe them. Do not try to be a detective.

1. Listen and Believe

Listen without judging. Say clearly: "I believe you." Show you understand and trust them.

2. It Is Not Their Fault

Tell them clearly: The abuse is not your fault. No one deserves this.

3. Respect Their Choices

Support what they want to do. Do not force them to leave if they are not ready. Leaving can be very dangerous.

4. Offer Real Help

Ask about real needs: "Can I help with the kids?" or "Do you need a lift somewhere?"

5. Plan for Safety

Talk about ways to be safer (like packing an emergency bag). If you are not sure, call an expert.

6. Privacy

Promise to keep their story private. Only tell someone if there is a serious danger right now.

Helping Someone Who Used Violence

Violence can happen in any relationship. This includes women being violent to men, and violence in same-sex relationships. Your goal is to get them to expert help without making things unsafe.

⚠️ IMPORTANT SAFETY WARNING Do NOT ask for details about the violence. This can make it unsafe for the victim.

How to Act

Stay calm. Do not judge. Do not agree with their excuses. But do not start a fight.

Do Not Use These

NEVER use couples counselling, anger management, or mediation. These are dangerous here.

What to Say

"From what you have told me... it sounds like you are having a hard time. There are services available to help people change their behaviour."

Workplace Duties

Workplaces must keep people safe. You must support the person and follow the rules.

  • Find a Private Place
    Go to a private room now. Do not talk where others can hear.
  • Know Your Role
    Be supportive. But remember you are a colleague, not a counsellor.
  • Check Safety Now
    Is the violent person coming to work? Do you need to call security or police?
  • Make Helpful Changes
    Can you change their phone number? Change work hours? Move their desk away from the front door?
  • Write It Down
    Keep a private note of what was said and done. Keep this separate from other staff files.
  • Check the Rules
    Ask if they want to talk to HR about Domestic Violence Leave or support services.
