Why They Might Hesitate

Before someone discloses to you, they are often weighing up serious risks. Whether they are male, female, or non-binary, the fear of judgment is universal.

"Will I be judged?"

They worry you might see them differently, blame them for staying, or talk about them behind their back. They need to know you are non-judgmental.

"Will I lose my job?"

They fear that disclosing might limit their career options or that they will be seen as "too much drama" for the workplace.

"Can I trust you?"

They are watching to see if you can keep a secret. They worry that if they tell you, the whole office will find out.

"Will you believe me?"

Men often fear being told to "toughen up," while women fear being called emotional. Everyone fears not being believed. They need to know you believe them unconditionally.

Are You Ready to Respond?

Before you have the conversation, consider if you are in the right headspace and environment.

Check the Environment

Is this the right place? Do not have this conversation in an open-plan office or a hallway. Ensure absolute privacy where you cannot be overheard.

Check Your Mindset

Are you calm? Are you ready to listen without trying to "fix" it? If you are stressed or busy, you might miss the signs.

Check Your Knowledge

Do you know where the policy is? Do you have the number for 1800RESPECT or EAP handy? You don't need to be an expert, just prepared.

Check Your Boundaries

Remember: You are a supporter, not a saviour. Your role is to walk beside them, not to lead the way or force them to act.
